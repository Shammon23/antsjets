Patio repointing for cracked joints and tired paving

Patio repointing is the work between the slabs. When old joints crack, wash out or grow weeds, the whole patio can look tired even after cleaning. Repointing fills those joints again for a neater, more finished surface.

You can call it patio repointing, patio pointing, paving repointing, slab joint repair or patio repair. Send photos and Ant’s Jets can advise the right next step.

  • Raking out failed or loose patio joints where needed.
  • Cleaning and preparing joints before new pointing.
  • Repointing patio slabs and garden paving with a neat finish.
  • Advice where slabs are loose, sunken or need repair before repointing.

FAQs

What is patio repointing?

Patio repointing means replacing or repairing the material between paving slabs. It is also called patio pointing, slab joint repair or paving repointing.

Do I need patio cleaning before repointing?

Often yes. Cleaning first helps show the condition of the joints and gives a better finished look.
