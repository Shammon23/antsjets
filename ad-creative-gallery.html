Creative quality rule

What changes after Sean's video feedback

The unrealistic water/cleaning mismatch is a hard reject. For videos, only use clips where the lance, water impact, dirty runoff and cleaned strip line up in the same place. For images, prefer static 50/50 before-and-after shots, carousel comparisons and real job composites because they do not have that motion realism problem.

  • Real photo composites first.
  • 50/50 split images over fake-looking moving water.
  • No “water sprays here, cleaning happens there” videos in main campaigns.
  • Use close-up clean-stripe images only if the clean line is physically believable.

Production prompt rule

For any new generated images

Make the surface, camera angle and lighting consistent. Use dry clean after-side, dirty mossy before-side, realistic UK paving, no impossible water spray, no warped bricks, no fake text, no over-shiny wet finish.

Extra test images

More image creatives to test by angle

Use these as test assets. Anything that looks unrealistic can be hidden with the button on the card. Keep the stronger realistic images in rotation and reject the weak ones before putting them into Meta or Google.

Video QA

Video creatives now sit below images until reviewed

Because Sean spotted realism issues in the AI video tests, do not lead campaigns with video unless the cleaning path is believable. Use images and real before/after composites first.
